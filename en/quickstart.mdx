<br />

Follow these steps to create a bot quickly in AI Studio.

## Prerequisites

- An active AI Studio account with **Admin** or **Developer** role
- A project where you can associate your bot

---

## Step 1: Open the Bots page

Log into AI Studio. You will be redirected to the **Bots** page automatically.

<Frame caption="AI Studio - Bots page">
  <img src="/images/bots-page.png" />
</Frame>

---

## Step 2: Create your bot

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click New bot">
    Click the **"New bot"** button in the left sidebar. Choose **"Create a bot from scratch"** or **"Use a template"**.

    <Frame caption="Create a new bot modal">
      <img src="/images/create-bot-modal.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose name and type">
    Enter a **Bot Name** and select a **Bot Type**:

    <Frame caption="Selecting a bot type">
      <img src="/images/bot-type-select.png" />
    </Frame>

    Select **VoiceAgent** as the bot type. This is an AI-powered voice agent with LLM, ideal for phone-based conversational experiences.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Create">
    Your bot is created with version `00001` and tag <span style={{backgroundColor: '#722ED1', color: 'white', padding: '2px 8px', borderRadius: '10px', fontSize: '12px'}}>Dev</span> by default. It will appear in the sidebar ready to configure.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Step 3: Add nodes to the flow

Click **"Add Node"** in the toolbar. Each node is a step in the conversation. Configure the node's messages and functions in the side panel.

<Frame caption="Editing a VoiceAgent node">
  <img src="/images/bot-node.png" />
</Frame>

---

## Step 4: Configure voice and LLM

Click **"Bot Config"** in the toolbar to set up:

- **TTS** — Voice provider and voice selection
- **STT** — Speech recognition provider and language
- **LLM** — AI model provider and temperature

---

## Step 5: Save

Click **"Save"** in the toolbar. Your bot is ready.

---

## Step 6: Test and review

Once your bot is saved, run a test call. To run your bot at scale with a list of contacts, go to the [Campaigns](/en/campaigns/create-campaign) section where you can upload a CSV and execute batch calls.

After a call, navigate to **Conversations** to review how it went.

<Frame caption="Conversations page">
  <img src="/images/conversations-page.png" />
</Frame>

There you can:

- Listen to the **call recording**
- Read the full **transcript** (assistant, user, system, and tool messages)
- Check **performance metrics** (latency, tokens, TTS)
- View the **parameters and state** of the conversation

<Frame caption="Conversation detail">
  <img src="/images/conversation-details.png" />
</Frame>
